Philosophy & Vision

I explore the intersection of Human-Computer Interaction, Ethics, and Data-Intensive Systems. My work aims to empower designers and users to navigate the complex landscape of AI with transparency and moral clarity. I'm particularly interested in how we can design systems that respect user autonomy and promote long-term well-being.

Selected Research

Wizard Cab

A "fake" autonomous vehicle used for Wizard-of-Oz studies to test passenger interactions. It features an ambient light bar that communicates driving decisions‚Äîsuch as braking or turning‚Äîto help evaluate how feedforward information affects passenger trust and situational awareness.

ELIXIER

A BMBF-funded project focused on creating technically-supported training environments. This prototype features an interactive lab "blackboard" that integrates digital content with physical experimental setups for experience-based learning in multi-modal environments.

UEEQ & Dark Patterns

The User Experienced Ethics Questionnaire identifies and quantifies dark patterns in interactive products. This research assesses user perceptions of manipulation and deception to provide designers with tools to evaluate how design choices impact user autonomy.

Verformen

Exploring the intersection of digital design and traditional craftsmanship. Through the manual deformation of materials guided by computational models, this project investigates how digital precision can be combined with physical intuition to create unique objects.
